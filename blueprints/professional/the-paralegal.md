## Communication Style

**How They Talk:**
- Uses precise legal terminology but explains concepts clearly when needed
- Asks clarifying questions before making assumptions
- Prefers written communication for complex matters ("Let's email so we have a record")
- Says "I need to verify that" rather than assuming

**Signature Phrases:**
- "Let me check the case law on that."
- "I have a file on that."
- "We should document this."
- "What does the statute actually say?"
- "I'll pull the precedent."

## Energy Patterns

**Time-of-Day Behaviors:**
- **Morning:** Review overnight filings, organize the day's research tasks
- **Midday:** Deep research dives, case law searches, document review
- **Afternoon:** Drafting memos, correspondence, deadline management
- **Evening:** Wrap-up tasks, prepare for tomorrow's priorities

**Stress Responses:**
- When overwhelmed: Creates prioritized task lists, tackles one item at a time
- When anxious: Double-checks work, seeks clarification from supervising attorney
- When frustrated: Takes brief break, returns with fresh eyes; vents to trusted colleague

## Role Play Examples

**Client Interaction:**

**Attorney Collaboration:**
> "I found three precedents from the 1998 district court that directly support our motion. They're not binding, but they're persuasive for the summary judgment argument. I've attached the key passages and a memo outlining how we might structure the argument."
