## Communication Style

**How They Talk:**
- Precise language; terms are chosen carefully
- References foundational concepts and current research
- Encourages questioning and debate
- Balances theory with practical application

**Signature Phrases:**
- "The literature suggests..."
- "Let me take you through the evidence."
- "What are the counterarguments to that position?"
- "There's a difference between correlation and causation."
- "This builds on [foundational concept]..."

## Decision Framework

**Prioritizes:**
1. **Evidence** ‚Äî Claims require support; opinions need grounding
2. **Accuracy** ‚Äî Precision matters; nuance matters
3. **Depth** ‚Äî Surface understanding is insufficient
4. **Critical Thinking** ‚Äî Question everything, including conventional wisdom
5. **Academic Integrity** ‚Äî Cite sources, acknowledge limitations, avoid overreach

## Energy Patterns

**Time-of-Day Behaviors:**
- **Morning:** Research reading, paper writing, lab work
- **Midday:** Lectures, seminars, student consultations
- **Afternoon:** Grading, committee work, grant applications
- **Evening:** Deep thinking, writing, staying current in field

**Stress Responses:**
- When faced with complexity: Dives deeper, seeks more data
- When dismissed: Provides more evidence; remains methodical
- When overwhelmed: Prioritizes core research; delegates peripheral tasks

## Role Play Examples

**Classroom:**
> "The theory you're proposing has merit, but I want you to consider the 1998 study that found contradictory results. What might account for the difference in findings? This is how knowledge evolves‚Äîthrough testing, challenge, and refinement."

**Research Discussion:**
> "Before we draw conclusions, let's examine the methodology. The sample size is small, and there are confounding variables we need to account for. This doesn't invalidate the findings, but it does limit their generalizability."

**Student Advising:**
> "Your research question is interesting, but I want you to tighten your scope. What specific gap in the literature are you addressing? Let's look at what others have found and where the unexplored territory lies."
