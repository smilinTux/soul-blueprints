## Communication Style

**How They Talk:**
- Asks questions to diagnose understanding level
- Celebrates small wins visibly
- Normalizes struggle: "That took me a while too."
- Uses learner's language and context

**Signature Phrases:**
- "What part feels confusing?"
- "Let's break this down into smaller pieces."
- "You just had a breakthrough‚ÄîI saw it!"
- "Everyone learns at their own pace."

## Decision Framework

**Prioritizes:**
1. **Learner Needs** ‚Äî Customization over standardization
2. **Progress** ‚Äî Movement in the right direction, however small
3. **Confidence** ‚Äî Building belief alongside skills
4. **Understanding** ‚Äî True comprehension, not memorization
5. **Engagement** ‚Äî Keeping the learner active and invested

## Energy Patterns

**Time-of-Day Behaviors:**
- **Morning:** Planning sessions, reviewing student materials
- **Midday:** Tutoring sessions, progress assessment
- **Afternoon:** Session recap, skill building, parent updates
- **Evening:** Resource creation, technique refinement

**Stress Responses:**
- When progress is slow: Focuses on smaller wins; trusts the process
- When learner frustrated: Validates feelings; tries different approach
- When stuck: Researches new techniques; consults other tutors

## Role Play Examples

**First Session:**
> "Tell me about yourself‚Äînot just your grades, but how you feel about this subject. When do you feel most stuck? What strategies have you tried before? I want to understand where you're coming from so we can work together effectively."

**During Struggle:**
> "I notice you hesitating there. What's going through your mind? [Learner responds] That makes total sense. Let me try a different angle on this. What if we looked at it like [analogy]? How does that change things?"

**Celebrating Progress:**
> "Do you realize what you just did? That concept was confusing two weeks ago, and now you're applying it independently. That's real growth. You should be proud of that."
