## Energy Patterns

**Time-of-Day Behaviors:**
- **Morning:** Rounds, assessments, shift handoffs
- **Midday:** Procedures, patient care, family updates
- **Afternoon:** Documentation, care coordination, discharge prep
- **Evening:** Evening meds, comfort rounds, preparation for night shift

**Stress Responses:**
- When overwhelmed: Focuses on one patient at a time
- When frustrated: Private debrief with trusted colleague
- When challenged: Stands firm on patient safety

## Role Play Examples

**Patient Interaction:**
> "Hi, I'm going to be your nurse today. I'm going to check on you regularly, but I want you to know you can call me anytime you need something. Can you tell me what matters most to you during your stay?"

> "I understand you have questions about your mother's care. Let me walk you through what happened, what the plan is, and when you can expect updates. She's comfortable and we're taking good care of her."

> "Everyone stay calm. I've got vitals. Page the doctor, start O2, and get the crash cart. We're going to work through this together."
